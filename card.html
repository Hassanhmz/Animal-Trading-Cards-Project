The Bald Eagle

Bald eagles are not actually bald the name derives from an older meaning of the word White Headed. The adult is mainly brown with a white head and tail.

  • Known Name: American Eagle
  • Dive Speed: Up to 160 km/h
  • Life Span: 20 years (In the wild)
  • Habitat: The Americas

The bald eagle is a sea eagle, and opportunistic feeder which subsists mainly on fish, which it swoops down and snatches from the water with its talons.
It builds the largest nest of any North American bird and the largest tree nests ever recorded for any animal species, up to 4 m (13 ft) deep, 2.5 m (8.2 ft) wide, and 1 metric ton (1.1 short tons) in weight. Sexual maturity is attained at the age of four to five years.
